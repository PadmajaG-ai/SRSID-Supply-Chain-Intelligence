. Operational Performance (The "Reliability" Metrics)
These track how well the vendor actually does their job day-to-day.

On-Time Delivery (OTD): The percentage of orders delivered on or before the promised date. In Ariba, this is often pulled from Goods Receipt (GR) data.

Supplier Defect Rate: The ratio of substandard products or service failures to the total units/events delivered (measured in PPM—Parts Per Million).

Lead Time Variability: Not just how fast they are, but how predictable they are. High variability ruins your production or project scheduling.

Order Accuracy: The percentage of orders delivered with the correct quantity, items, and documentation (matching the PO).

2. Financial & Strategic Metrics (The "Value" Metrics)
These measure the economic impact of the partnership.

Purchase Price Variance (PPV): The difference between the standard/contracted price and the actual price paid.

Contract Compliance Rate: The percentage of spend that stays within the negotiated contract terms. This is critical for preventing "Maverick Spend."

Procurement ROI: A comparison of the total cost of managing the vendor vs. the savings and value they generate.

Innovation Contribution: A qualitative score tracking how often the vendor suggests cost-saving designs, sustainable materials, or process improvements.

3. Risk & Compliance (The "Resilience" Metrics)
Essential for a Supplier Risk Dashboard, which you’ve expressed interest in building.

Financial Stability Score: Uses credit ratings (like D&B or RapidRatings) to predict the likelihood of vendor bankruptcy.

Cybersecurity Posture: Assessment of their data protection standards (ISO 27001, SOC 2) and their history of data breaches.

ESG & Sustainability Score: Measurement of their environmental impact, labor practices, and ethical governance—increasingly a legal requirement in many regions.

Concentration Risk: How much of your critical business depends on this one vendor? Over-reliance creates a single point of failure.
